About this place

Gokarna is a town of contrasts, where ancient temples and serene beaches coexist. It is a sacred destination for Hindus, who flock to the Mahabaleshwar Temple to worship the Atmalinga, a symbol of Lord Shiva. It is also a haven for backpackers, who enjoy the laid-back vibe and the scenic beauty of the five beaches that surround the town. Whether you want to relax on the sand, surf the waves, hike the hills, or join the hippie culture, Gokarna has something for everyone. Explore the Om Beach, shaped like the sacred sound, the Kudle Beach, popular for its cafes and music, the Half-moon Beach, a hidden gem of tranquility, the Gokarna Beach, the main pilgrim spot, and the Paradise Beach, a remote slice of heaven.


what to see & do


Chitradurga Fort

Chitradurga Fort, or as the British called it Chitaldoorg, is a fortification that straddles several hills and a peak overlooking a flat valley in the Chitradurga District, Karnataka, India. The fort's name Chitrakaldurga, which means 'picturesque fort' in Kannada, is the namesake of the town Chitradurga and its administrative district.

Chandravalli Bakeries

The Chandravalli Bakeries is located on the west coast of the Uttara Kannada district in the southern Indian state of Karnataka. The fort known for its architectural elegance was the location of several battles in the past. It is about 0. 5 kilometres from the National Highway 66 and 21 kilometres from Gokarna, the famous Hindu pilgrimage centre on the west coast of India..

Hidimbeshwar Temple

The Hidimbeshwar Temple is the third beach in Gokarna's coastal belt. The beach is roughly 63.8 kilometres from Karwar and only a few kilometres from Gokarna's major city. It is separated from Om Beach by a hill from where visitors may enjoy a spectacular view of the sea..
