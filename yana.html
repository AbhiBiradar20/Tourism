About this place

Yana is a town of contrasts, where ancient temples and serene beaches coexist. It is a sacred destination for Hindus, who flock to the Mahabaleshwar Temple to worship the Atmalinga, a symbol of Lord Shiva. It is also a haven for backpackers, who enjoy the laid-back vibe and the scenic beauty of the five beaches that surround the town. Whether you want to relax on the sand, surf the waves, hike the hills, or join the hippie culture, Gokarna has something for everyone. Explore the Om Beach, shaped like the sacred sound, the Kudle Beach, popular for its cafes and music, the Half-moon Beach, a hidden gem of tranquility, the Gokarna Beach, the main pilgrim spot, and the Paradise Beach, a remote slice of heaven.


what to see & do


Dabbe Falls

Dabbe Falls, one of Gokarna's five beaches, is a popular tourist attraction of Karnataka if you're looking for serenity and a chance to get away from the bustle of city life. The beach, which is surrounded on three sides by hills, is completely isolated and abandoned for most of the year.

Netrani Island

The chairman of the Netrani Group, Netrani Ganesh, founded the organization in 2009. The goal of Netrani Ganesh’s scuba diving firm is to offer affordable underwater diving experiences in India. At present, Netrani Adventures is the largest water sports and scuba diving center in India.

Half Moon Beach

The lovely Half-Moon beach is the third beach in Gokarna's coastal belt. The beach is roughly 63.8 kilometres from Karwar and only a few kilometres from Gokarna's major city. It is separated from Om Beach by a hill from where visitors may enjoy a spectacular view of the sea..
