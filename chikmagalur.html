About this place

Chikmagalur, or Chikkamagaluru, is a scenic hill station in Karnataka, India, that offers a variety of attractions for nature lovers, history buffs, and adventure seekers. You can explore the rich heritage of the Hoysala dynasty in the temples of Belavadi and Angadi, or admire the panoramic views from the hill forts of Ballalarayana Durga and Kemmangundi. You can also enjoy the wildlife and biodiversity of the Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary, or trek to the highest peaks of the Western Ghats in the Baba Budan Giri Range and Mullayanagiri. Chikmagalur is also known for its coffee plantations and the Deviramma temple, where the local goddess is worshipped with fervor.


what to see & do


Mullayyana Giri

Mullayyanagiri is the highest peak in Karnataka, India. Mullayyanagiri is located in the Chandra Dhrona Hill Ranges of the Western Ghats of Chikkamagaluru Taluk. With a height of 1,925 metres, it is the highest peak in Karnataka and also the 23rd highest peak in Western Ghats. The summit of Mullayanagiri has a small temple and houses a police radio relay station. Seethalayyanagiri is a prominent peak which is adjacent to this place.

Baba Budangiri

The trek to Baba Budangiri is a moderate level trek that spans a distance of 9 to 10 kilometers and can be completed in approximately 5 – 6 hours and can be completed in 2 phases. The best time for the trek is anytime between September to March as the weather remains mostly pleasant during the day.
